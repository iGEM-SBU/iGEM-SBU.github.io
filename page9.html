Attributions

iGEM at Stony Brook would like to thank the following people and organizations for their hand in helping us realize our project.

iGEM advisors - J. Peter Gergen, Joshua Rest, Gabor Balazsi, Jarrod French, Steven Glynn
  • Gave advice regarding project development and project proposals
  • Helped with designing the constructs 
iGEM Reading Course occurred Spring of 2018 from February 2, 2018 until May 11, 2018
  • Involved TAs from the previous iGEM teams including: Nick Roig, Brian Yang, Chloe Savino, Chris Guiliano, Fatima Maqsood, Julianna Casella, Lyle Suh, Megan Schiesser, Ridee Raquib, Ryan Kawlerski, Timothy Darby, Jerin Thomas
  • Reading course was used for project development and getting to know what the iGEM competition is
  •  Every week, we had TAs who presented on what iGEM was and mentored us through our project development
  • We also had 14 theory words which we were assigned to and presented to the team every week 
  • We split into groups and individually researched potentially projects that we’re interested in 
  • With each week, we would present on our own projects and then vote on the top 4, top 3, and eventually, filtered down to two main projects
  • We finalized our project by the end of the reading course
  • Advisors came in during some of our presentations to give us advice
  • Some TAs guided us and provided resources after the reading course as well
  • During the summer, the TAs would come into lab and help us with any problems with had such as interlab, gel purification, etc.
