Attributions

iGEM at Stony Brook would like to thank the following people and organizations for their hand in helping us realize our project.

Advisors

Our project advisors: Dr. J. Peter Gergen, Dr. Joshua Rest, Dr. Gabor Balazsi, Dr. Jarrod French, and Dr. Steven Glynn, for all their advice and guidance throughout the entirety of our project along with help in designing our constructs

iGEM Reading Course 

The previous iGEM teams who were our TA’s, specifically including: Nick Roig, Brian Yang, Chloe Savino, Chris Guiliano, Fatima Maqsood, Julianna Casella, Lyle Suh, Megan Schiesser, Ridee Raquib, Ryan Kawlerski, Timothy Darby, Jerin Thomas. Through the reading course, our team learned about the iGEM competition and the expectations associated with it. The weekly meetings were used to create a project and develop it through the help of the TA’s who presented on what iGEM was and imparted their knowledge and experience. We are thankful for the TA’s continuing to aid us through the summer when needed!

Nancy Black -

Cyanobacteria Support

John Aikens -
President of Mesodynamics and Principle of Aikens Associates with a PhD in Chemistry, helped us with our plasmid, E.coli, and aided in troubleshooting our initial project idea by anticipating issues we had not thought of.  
Michael Axelrod - 
Greenhouse Curator and Instructional Support Specialist, helped us culture cyanobacteria. 
Brian Baynes - 
Entrepreneur in sustainability and synthetic biology with a PhD in Chemical Engineering, discussed with us various experimental strategies as well as the challenges of using cyanobacteria to produce alkanes. He helped develop the real world application of the project with regards to biofuel and environmental plastic.
Jackie Collier- 
Associate Professor with PhD in Biological Sciences, helped with growing and culturing our cyanobacteria and showed us her set up so that we could see how cyanobacteria is usually grown. She helped us grow our initial cultures of cyanobacteria while we were still getting set up in our lab for the summer.
Daniel Ducat -
Assistant Professor with a PhD in biology, helped us with constructing plasmids and overall work with cyanobacteria.
Susan Golden - 
Professor with PhD in genetics, offered advice regarding the luciferase promoters and answered our many questions about her cyanobacterial vectors.
Mckenna Hicks -
Former member of the 2017 UCSC iGEM Team, answered our questions in regards to her project last year which involved cyanobacteria, she helped us with our constructs and culturing techniques. 
Hung Li (Martin) -
PhD student of Professor Hu, helped us in deciding whether to use CRISPR-Cas9 in PCC 7942.
Monique Richards -
Graduate student of Yale University and our Promega representative, helped us in ordering our Promega products and answered all questions pertaining the products. She also helped us troubleshoot any issues with Promega.
